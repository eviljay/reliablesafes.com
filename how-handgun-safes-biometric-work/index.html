In general, biometric security recognition system uses two main categories of biometric characteristics; the physiological characteristics (fingerprints, DNA, face, iris, palm print, etc.) and the behavioral characteristics like voice tone. Handgun safes biometric scanner primarily uses fingerprints as its distinguishing mark. More advanced biometric scanners may use iris or voice. Since the system makes use of your unique distinguishing marks, it gives you exclusive access to storage devices or cases like handgun safes.

For the system to work, you first need to input your unique profile into the system. For instance, using your handgun safes biometric scanner for the first time allows you to store your fingerprint, iris or voice into the system. This way, the device will store this security access information. With your continued use, the scanner will continuously detect and compare your scans to the information or patterns that was initially stored in its biometric database. In this light, every time someone attempts to open the safe, the system will run its identification mode and will only allow access if the scanned profile matches what the system has initially stored.
