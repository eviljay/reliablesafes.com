There are many different kinds of locking mechanisms for safes, from key entry, electronic key coding, as well as fingerprint access. While there are pros and cons to all of these safes, the biometric or fingerprint safes are quickly becoming recognized as one of the most secure.

Regardless of your choice in locking mechanisms be it a fingerprint pistol safe or a wall safe having a bedside handgun safe is the most direct option for quick access. Aside from biometric safes your options are still very secure and often cheaper. Tumbler combination locks are the classic option, and a more recent development known as an electronic keypad has quickly become the preference of many. If you do choose to purchase a combination lock remember that instant access to your safe in an emergency, when it is most difficult to remember things such as a combination, is very important. Memorize your combination clearly and use your safe often to gain the muscle memory of accessing it. This can mean the difference between life and death in some circumstances.
